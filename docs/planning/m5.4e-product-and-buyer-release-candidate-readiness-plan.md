## Product And Buyer Readiness Principles

Product and buyer release-candidate planning follows these principles:

- Buyer-facing narrative must match proven product behavior and current proof boundaries.
- Demo stories must distinguish controlled demo capability from pilot, hosted, operational, security, and production proof.
- Release-candidate packaging requires later AP approval and evidence; planning alone cannot create release-candidate status.
- Claims must map to evidence, and stronger claims must remain blocked until future proof exists.
- Product positioning must not imply unsupported compliance, production, hosted, operational, security, tenant-isolation, or release-candidate readiness.
- Buyer-facing artifacts must include known limitations, unresolved proof boundaries, and the evidence status needed to strengthen claims.
- Demo data and evidence summaries must remain sanitized and must not expose sensitive, raw, hosted, target, environment, auth, claim, payload, or machine-specific values.
- Reviewers must approve claim wording, demo boundaries, limitation disclosures, and release-candidate packaging scope before any packaging or buyer-facing release-candidate use.
- AI must not decide final scores, gates, risk tiers, approvals, recommendations, compliance status, regulated decisions, or release-candidate approval.
- M5.4e does not bypass the M5.4b security/governance plan, M5.4c hosted evidence gate, or M5.4d deployment and operations plan.

## Product And Buyer Readiness Planning Domains

### Buyer Story And Target Persona

The buyer story should center on governed delivery: assessing before automating, governing before execution, human approval of risk, and evidence-backed decisions. The target persona should be described by role and responsibility, such as enterprise operations, transformation, governance, security, or delivery leadership.

Future buyer-facing use must avoid claims that exceed verified evidence. M5.4e does not approve buyer-readiness claims.

### Canonical Demo Story And Safe Demo Boundaries

The canonical demo story should show the governed path from organization setup through process assessment, deterministic scoring, recommendation review, decision pack, governance handoff, document generation, work item creation, delivery board tracking, and monitor views.

Demo boundaries must state that demo behavior is controlled product demonstration, not proof of hosted readiness, production readiness, tenant isolation, security readiness, deployment readiness, operational readiness, or release-candidate readiness.

### Product Positioning And Enterprise Narrative

The enterprise narrative should describe AvalaOS Core as a governed AI and automation delivery platform with deterministic scoring, human approval, evidence records, and scoped governance and delivery surfaces.

Positioning must avoid unsupported compliance, production, security, hosted, operational, and release-candidate claims until future evidence proves them.

### Module Narrative And Lifecycle Explanation

Future buyer-facing artifacts should explain the lifecycle across Avala Assess, Avala Govern Lite, Avala Studio, Avala Delivery Lite, and Avala Monitor as a governed handoff sequence.

The module narrative must distinguish current scoped product behavior from future proof requirements for hosted validation, deployment, operations, tenant isolation, and release-candidate packaging.

### Proof-To-Claim Mapping

Every claim must map to a proof source, accepted evidence record, or explicit limitation. When proof is absent, the claim must be blocked or downgraded to a planning statement.

Future proof-to-claim mapping must include claim owner, evidence source, allowed wording, blocked wording, reviewer, and AP approval status.

### Allowed Claims Vs Blocked Claims

Allowed claims may describe planning outputs, accepted docs-only gates, deterministic scoring controls, human approval principles, and current demo boundaries when supported by evidence.

Blocked claims include any statement that implies schema readiness, RLS readiness, RLS helper readiness, artifact SELECT isolation, tenant isolation, hosted readiness, production readiness, local startup success, deployment readiness, operational readiness, security readiness, buyer readiness, product readiness, or release-candidate readiness.

### Demo Data And Evidence Redaction Expectations

Demo data must be clearly treated as demo or sanitized sample data. Buyer-facing evidence must use summarized proof references and must not disclose sensitive data, raw payloads, target values, environment values, auth material, claim values, logs, stdout, stderr, stack traces, local paths, or machine-specific values.

Evidence redaction expectations from M5.4b, M5.4c, and M5.4d remain controlling.

### Release-Candidate Packaging Prerequisites, Without Packaging

Future release-candidate packaging requires accepted M5.4e closure, explicit AP approval, exact packaging scope, claim map approval, limitation disclosure, required evidence list, reviewer approval, and storage boundary.

M5.4e does not package, label, archive, tag, publish, or otherwise prepare a release candidate.

### Sales/Buyer-Facing Artifact Expectations

Future sales or buyer-facing artifacts should include product overview, canonical demo narrative, module lifecycle explanation, proof-to-claim map, limitation disclosure, buyer FAQ, evidence summary, and next-proof requirements.

Artifacts must be reviewed for unsupported claims before use.

### Risk, Limitation, And Unresolved-Proof Disclosure Model

Future buyer-facing materials must disclose unresolved proof boundaries in plain language. Limitations must distinguish planning status from verified behavior and must avoid implying that unresolved areas are already proven.

Disclosure must cover local DB unresolved status, schema not proven, RLS not proven, tenant isolation not newly verified, hosted readiness not proven, deployment readiness not proven, operational readiness not proven, security readiness not proven, and release-candidate readiness not proven.

### Reviewer And Approval Model Before Release-Candidate Packaging

Before release-candidate packaging, reviewers must approve the claim map, demo boundaries, limitation disclosures, evidence references, packaging scope, distribution boundary, and stop conditions.

AP approval is required before packaging, buyer-facing release-candidate use, or strengthening blocked claims.

### Dependency On M5.4b, M5.4c, And M5.4d Boundaries

M5.4e depends on M5.4b for security and governance planning boundaries, M5.4c for hosted evidence planning boundaries, and M5.4d for deployment and operations planning boundaries.

M5.4e cannot override or weaken those boundaries. Any future enterprise release-candidate evidence pack must preserve their approval, redaction, stop-condition, and proof requirements.
