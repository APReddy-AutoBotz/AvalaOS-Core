# M5.4f Enterprise Release Candidate Evidence Pack

## Purpose

M5.4f assembles the accepted M5.4a through M5.4e planning and closure evidence into a controlled enterprise release-candidate evidence pack.

This pack separates accepted docs-only planning evidence from unproven readiness claims. It records what M5.4 planned, what M5.4 did not prove, what claims remain blocked, and what future AP-approved proof is required before any release-candidate readiness, hosted readiness, production readiness, deployment readiness, security readiness, product readiness, or buyer readiness claim.

M5.4f is evidence-pack assembly only. It does not package a release candidate, perform deployment, perform hosted validation, execute DB/RLS/artifact checks, change runtime behavior, inspect schema, or produce real readiness evidence.

## Future Proof Requirements

### Security/Governance Proof

Future security and governance proof must show identity, role, organization, workspace, tenant, least-privilege, audit, retention, evidence-redaction, data-classification, and provider-secret governance under AP-approved scope. It must not expose secrets, auth material, payloads, row data, claim values, target identifiers, raw logs, raw stdout, raw stderr, stack traces, local paths, or machine-specific values.

### Hosted Evidence Proof

Future hosted evidence proof must use the M5.4c approval gate. It must identify approved assertion names, target class, run count, allowed sanitized result fields, stop conditions, reviewer, evidence storage boundary, and prohibited output policy before execution.

### Deployment/Operations Proof

Future deployment and operations proof must use the M5.4d planning model. It must prove release approval, environment class, change-control expectations, required checks, rollback ownership, incident ownership, observability expectations, backup/restore expectations, access review, support handoff, and sanitized operational evidence under AP-approved scope.

### Product/Buyer Release-Candidate Proof

Future product and buyer proof must use the M5.4e claim-control model. It must prove an approved claim map, demo boundaries, limitation disclosures, reviewer signoff, artifact set, packaging scope, distribution boundary, and stop conditions before any release-candidate packaging or buyer-facing release-candidate use.

### DB/RLS/Artifact Isolation Proof

Future DB/RLS/artifact proof must remain behind an explicit AP approval gate. It must define exact assertions for schema, RLS policy behavior, helper behavior, same-tenant behavior, cross-tenant negative behavior, artifact access, allowed sanitized results, prohibited outputs, fail-closed conditions, and reviewer signoff before execution.
